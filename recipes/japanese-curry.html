Japanese Curry

Description

Japanese curry is different from Indian or Thai curries. It is more of a brown stew and it can be mild or spicy, depending on your tastes. The curry roux, from no heat to very spicy, can be bought at any international grocery store.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 ¾ pounds beef chuck or chicken thighs, cut into cubes
  • 3 onions, diced
  • 1 tablespoon ketchup
  • 1 ½ teaspoons Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 pinch cayenne pepper, or to taste (Optional)
  • 4 carrots, cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 3 medium potatoes, cut into 3-inch chunks
  • 1 cube chicken bouillon
  • water to cover

Steps

  1. Heat oil in a 6-quart pot over medium-high heat.
  2. Add meat and saute until brown, 5 to 7 minutes.
  3. Add onions and cook until starting to soften, about 3 minutes.
  4. Add ketchup and Worcestershire sauce. Stir to coat.
  5. Add cayenne pepper. Pour in water to cover mixture by 1 or 2 inches.
  6. Add carrots and bouillon. Simmer, skimming fat off the surface of the broth as needed, for 30 minutes.
  7. Add potatoes. Stir in 1 package of curry roux and let dissolve; add remaining curry as needed to achieve desired thickness.
  8. Continue simmering until beef and vegetables are tender, about 30 minutes more.
  9. Serve over white rice.
